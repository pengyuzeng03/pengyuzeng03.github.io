    • Research Experience

  • 1. Secure Analog Computing

    • Tags: Process Variation, Analog Compute-in-Memory, Hardware Security, Compressed Sensing, Automation

    Abstract: Although digital signal has dominated the modern computing system, analog computing has a great potential in neural network inference/training, solving partial differential equations and many other kinds of computations. Besides, the stochastic nature of analog circuits provides entropy source which can be used to achieve secure computing and communication.

    We proposed a novel edge computing structure combining analog compute-in-memory and physical unclonable function(PUF) on the same hardware and implement privacy-preserved compressing algorithm in analog domain. My research also conduct comprehensive quantitative analysis of the nonidealities in CIM. The fusiform impact (Right Figure) of process variation, which is common in SRAM and NVM-based analog computing system, is one of the main factors limiting the accuracy. A circuits and testbench generator is built in the research to achieve an automated design flow. [PDF] [Code]


  • 2. Pipelined ADC

    • Tags: ADC, Mismatch, Correction, Calibration, Agile Design, MATLAB

    Abstract: Pipelined ADC is one of the most popular types of analog-to-digital convertor (ADC) and the digital error correction is a significant algorithm in the pipelined ADC. The structure of the ADC depends on this algorithm which can correct the digital output error caused by the mismatch of comparators and aperture error. My research can be divided into three parts: analyze the algorithm, build a behavioral model of the ADC and design the circuits. [Slides]


  • 3. A 40Gb/s CTLE for a PAM4 Wireline Receiver

    • Tags: CTLE, PAM-4, ISI, Wireline, Transceiver, Cadence

    Abstract: As the data rate in the wireline transmission continues to increase, serializer & deserializer (SerDes) is widely used to recover the distorted signal caused by the channel with the low-pass feature. Continuous time linear equalizer (CTLE) as the frontend in the receiver plays a significant role in preliminary improving the signal quality. This research analyses the structure of CTLE and proposes comprehensive improvements on raising the peaking gain and keep the balance among the DC gain, power dissipation, silicon area, etc. [PDF]


  • 4. Digital and Board Level Circuits Design

    • Tags: SoC, Accelerator, ARM, FPGA, PCB

    Abstract: A SoC for real time image dehaze is implemented on Xilinx FPGA and using ARM Cortex-M3 as CPU (Left). The algorithm is divided into six steps and two 3-stage pipelines to accelerate the operation. The parallel filtering is also proposed to make full use of hardware resource and speed up the operation. PCB board is useful for chips verification and system implementation. I am familiar with PCB-level circuits design. These work (Right) is for a intelligent transport robot. [Schematics]
